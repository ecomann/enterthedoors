About The Doors

The Doors were an American blues rock formed in 1965 with 4 members (see later). There were considered to be one of the most influential but controversial rock bands to happen in the 1960s, mostly due to the band's frontman's poetic lyricism and iconic voice with his erratic stage persona.

The band took its name from a book named "The Doors of Perception", which itself is a reference to a quote by the English poet William Blake.

The Doors had a unique ability to combine poetic storytelling in their lyrics with uplifting, bouncy and energising blues rock, which both defined their sound from their very first but very successful self-titled album "The Doors" which was (and still is) widely considered to be one of the best rock albums of the 1960s, all the way to their last released album to date, "An American Prayer".
